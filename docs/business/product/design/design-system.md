## The Philosophy

**We don't sell brackets. We sell the feeling of being in a cup final every single week.**

Every decision in this document â€” every color, every word, every interaction â€” should be measured against one question: *Does this make the stakes feel real?*

Fantasy Premier League has millions of players. They're already engaged. They're already competitive. But most of them feel nothing. They're in 47th place. They'll never catch the leader. There's no drama.

We give them drama. We give them stakes. We give them one opponent, one week, win or go home.

That's what we're selling.

## Brand Pillars

### 1. Stakes
Everything has consequences. You advance or you're eliminated. There's no "pretty good" â€” there's only survival. Our design and language should always remind users what's on the line.

### 2. Drama
Every matchup is a story. David vs. Goliath. Revenge matches. Cinderella runs. We're not displaying data â€” we're narrating competition.

### 3. Prestige
This isn't a casual game. It's a championship. The Champions League doesn't apologize for being elite, and neither do we. Deep blues, gold accents, the feeling of walking into a final.

### 4. Clarity
When the stakes are real, you need to see clearly. The bracket interface is clean, scannable, ruthlessly functional. The drama comes from the competition, not the UI.

## Motion & Animation

### Duration Scale

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| `duration-fast` | 100ms | Hover states |
| `duration-normal` | 200ms | Clicks, interactions |
| `duration-slow` | 300ms | Panel slides |
| `duration-slower` | 500ms | Celebrations, modals |

### Easing

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| `ease-out` | `cubic-bezier(0, 0, 0.2, 1)` | Elements entering |
| `ease-in` | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1)` | Elements leaving |
| `ease-in-out` | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` | State changes |
| `ease-bounce` | `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` | Victories only |

### Animation Recipes

**Victory Celebration**
- Card scales to 1.02
- Gold shadow fades in
- Gold border pulses once
- Duration: 500ms, ease-bounce
- *This is earned. Make it feel good.*

**Elimination**
- Card fades to 50% opacity
- Subtle shake (optional)
- Duration: 300ms, ease-out
- *Quick and definitive. No lingering.*

**Advancement Line**
- Connector draws from winner to next round
- Duration: 300ms, ease-out
- *Show the path forward.*

**Score Update (Live)**
- Number scales to 1.1 briefly
- Flashes gold then settles
- Duration: 200ms
- *Every point matters.*

## Tournament Bracket Design

### Layout Principles

1. **Winners flow left to right.** The trophy is on the right.
2. **Connector lines show paths.** Gold for winners, gray for pending.
3. **User's path is highlighted.** They should instantly see where they are.
4. **Round headers tell the story.** "16 REMAIN" not "Round of 16"

- Stroke: 2px
- Default: Gray 200 (#E5E5E5)

- Corner radius: 4px
- Animation: Draw left-to-right on advancement (300ms, ease-out)

- Gold everything
- Trophy icon prominent

## Accessibility

### Requirements

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- 4.5:1 contrast for normal text
- 3:1 contrast for large text
- 44x44px minimum touch targets
- Visible focus states (gold outline)
- No information by color alone

### Focus States

Focus ring: 2px Gold (#C9A227), 2px offset

**Never remove focus outlines.** Style them, don't hide them.

### Screen Reader Considerations

- Matchup cards: "[Team A] [score] versus [Team B] [score]. [Team A] is winning by [X] points."
- Round headers: "[X] teams remaining"
- Winner announcements: "[Team] has advanced to [Round]"

### Color Independence

Always pair color with another indicator:
- Winner: Gold border + checkmark icon
- Eliminated: Dimmed + "ELIMINATED" text
- Live: Green border + "LIVE" badge
- Error: Red border + error icon + text
